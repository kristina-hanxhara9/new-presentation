🎯 Project Overview

Athana Data Science Services (ADSS) commissioned by the UK Department of Environment to develop machine learning models for predicting atmospheric emissions using the 2019 London Atmospheric Emissions Inventory (LAEI) dataset.

📊 LAEI 2019 Dataset Specifications

Comprehensive emissions data across London covering NOx, NO₂, PM₁₀, PM₂.₅, CO₂, SO₂, CH₄, and VOCs reported in tonnes/year by source type including transport, heating, industry, and aviation.

📍 Spatial Coverage

79,388 road segments analysed across all London boroughs and areas within the M25. Emissions mapped on 1km grids with 20m geographical resolution concentration data.

🔬 ML Methodology

Three complementary approaches: Ridge Regression for precision, Random Forest for feature analysis, K-means for geographical pattern discovery.

🔧 Data Preprocessing

StandardScaler normalisation, missing value imputation using geographical interpolation, and PCA for dimensionality reduction applied across all models.

📈 Traffic Metrics

Vehicle Kilometres (VKM), Annual Average Daily Traffic (AADT), and emission splits by vehicle type including exhaust and brake wear sources.

🎯 Target Variables

Focus on NOx emissions prediction with comprehensive analysis of PM₁₀, PM₂.₅, and CO₂ patterns across London's transport network.

🚗 Vehicle Type Emission Profiles

🚙 Passenger Cars Dominance

Passenger cars (petrol and diesel combined) represent the largest emission source, with petrol cars showing slightly higher individual contribution than diesel variants.

🚚 Commercial Vehicle Impact

Light Goods Vehicles (LGV) and Heavy Goods Vehicles (HGV) show significant contributions, particularly diesel variants which dominate commercial transport emissions.

🔬 Key Research Findings

🎯 Model Accuracy Achievement

Ridge Regression achieved 99.99% accuracy for NOx emission prediction with sub-kilogram annual error rates across 143,976 observations.

🚗 Passenger Car Dominance

Passenger cars identified as primary emission driver with 87.27% importance in Random Forest and 30.13% coefficient weight in Ridge analysis.

📍 Geographical Distribution Pattern

43.3% of London roads generate 31x higher emissions than baseline, revealing clear geographic clustering with 89.9% of roads in high/medium clusters.

⛽ Fuel Type Significance

Diesel vehicles show consistently higher coefficients across vehicle categories, with cold start effects contributing 18.40% combined impact.

🔧 Method Complementarity

Three ML approaches provide different insights: Ridge for precision (100% R²), Random Forest for relationships (97.36% R²), K-means for geography.

🏙️ Borough Variation

Significant variation across boroughs with Bexley leading NOx (1,973 tonnes) and Barnet leading PM₁₀ (324 tonnes) emissions.
